10. Cumulative assessments help evaluate mastery of skills.
Assessment of phonics skills must be done over an extended period of time to ensure mastery. Weekly assessments focusing on one skill often give “false positives.” That is, they show movement toward learning, but not transfer. If the skill isn’t worked on in subsequent weeks, learning can decay. Cumulative assessments help you determine which skills have truly been mastered. Make course corrections and adjust small-group differentiated support based on your findings.
Benchmark Phonics instruction includes cumulative assessments in the Teacher’s Resource System. The assessments include spelling, dictation, and word fluency, and provide information about mastery and decayed learning to support informed instructional decisions.
