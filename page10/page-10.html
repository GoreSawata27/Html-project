Cumulative Assessments
Catch instructional needs before they become instructional issues.
Cumulative Unit and Weekly Assessments are formative assessments for monitoring student progress on the week’s phonics elements and 4–6 weeks of previously taught phonics elements. The cumulative assessments inform instruction for differentiation in whole-group and small-group lessons. Quick and Easy to Administer
Cumulative Unit and Weekly Assessments are formative assessments for monitoring student progress on the week’s phonics elements and 4–6 weeks of previously taught phonics elements. The cumulative assessments inform instruction for differentiation in whole-group and small-group lessons. Check 5–6 students at the end of each week to continue monitoring mastery and transfer of spelling patterns taught and reviewed across multiple weeks.
Cumulative Assessments are designed to be time-efficient.
How to administer:
  • Each week, meet with 5–6 students to administer the cumulative assessment.
  • Meet with a different group of 5–6 students to administer the assessment during the next week.
  • Continue meeting with groups of 5–6 students each week until you’ve assessed every student.
  • Repeat.
The assessments include phonics elements from 4–6 weeks of previously taught skills, which allows you to monitor student progress effectively without having to assess each student every week.
Continually assessing a skill in subsequent weeks after explicit instruction will determine whether learning and transfer have truly been achieved. Student performance will determine how to pace Spiral Review lessons and how to form small groups.
Clearly Identified Next Steps
Each cumulative assessment includes actionable next steps for instruction, including strategies for forming small groups and modifying instruction, plus additional practice opportunities that will help students make gains.
Provides teachers with support in how to use assessment information along with next steps for instruction
