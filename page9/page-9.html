Phonics Manipulatives
Manipulatives engage students with a variety of learning modalities and create a hands-on learning environment.
Picture Word Cards are used to introduce a key word for the new sound. The front and back option allows teachers to show the printed word or just the picture.
Sound-Spelling Cards provide a key image and word to help students make connections to the sound and recognize the different spelling patterns made by a single sound. The back of the Sound-Spelling Cards provides rhymes, word lists, and other support for the teacher. Large cards are provided for classroom display. Smaller cards are provided with the corresponding unit for instruction.
Sound Wall Cards give students an opportunity to see and understand correct lenticular motions and copy them with their own mouths. Use the Sound Wall Cards to create a sound wall in your classroom. Each card includes a link to the corresponding articulation video.
Frieze Cards provide other words and pictures of a phonic element. These may be displayed in the classroom for daily use. Additional words are listed on the back of each Frieze Card.
Letter Cards are used to build words in pocket charts and Elkonin boxes. Multiple sets of cards are provided for each letter for use during small-group instruction and independent practice.
High-Frequency Word Cards are used in daily lessons to introduce, practice, and review to build automaticity of words found in multiple texts.
Write-On/Wipe-Off Workmats are referenced throughout the lessons to be used as Elkonin boxes, individual whiteboards for spelling and dictation, and more.
