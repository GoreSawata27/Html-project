8. Don’t lose sight of the bigger picture.
Some curricula overemphasize phonics isolated skill work, while ignoring other key aspects of early reading needs (e.g., vocabulary and background knowledge-building) that are essential to long-term reading progress. These skills plant the seeds for comprehension as students encounter increasingly complex texts with words not commonly used in speaking and topics that require more extensive background knowledge.
Benchmark Phonics lessons also build vocabulary and knowledge. Students apply phonics skills in texts that are aligned to each unit’s topic and knowledge strand. They are exposed to texts through shared reading experiences that build content-rich vocabulary and background on each topic.
